About Me

Doctor, researcher, and builder working at the intersection of health systems and technology.

I am a Research Fellow at the Department of Health Care Management, Technische Universität Berlin, and a Research Fellow at the European Observatory on Health Systems and Policies, Berlin Hub. I joined both in October 2025, and currently contribute to Observatory projects including OBS-PACE, which focuses on cancer research, care, and policy across the European Union.

Before moving into health systems research, I worked as a medical doctor, first as an intern at Jawaharlal Nehru Hospital in Mauritius and Panyu Central Hospital in China, then in the public hospital system in Mauritius across several departments. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I was part of the national vaccination programme. That clinical experience seeing where systems work and where they fail is what drives my research today.

I came to research through practice. The questions I ask now are the ones I couldn't answer at the bedside.

My research interests sit at the intersection of cancer care, mental health, health systems, digital health, and artificial intelligence in healthcare.
