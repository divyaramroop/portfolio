About This Series

AI is entering healthcare faster than most clinicians were trained to understand it. Tools that summarise patient notes, flag abnormalities in scans, suggest diagnoses, or assist in treatment planning are already in use — and more are coming.

But the conversation around how to use these tools safely is lagging behind. This series is an attempt to close that gap — written by a doctor, for anyone working in or alongside healthcare who wants to engage with AI thoughtfully.

The goal is not to slow down AI in medicine. It's to make sure we know what we're doing when we speed it up.

Each article in this series tackles one practical dimension of AI safety — from protecting sensitive data, to understanding what an AI actually does with your input, to knowing when not to trust a result. No technical background required.
